To build a language model, we teach it a very simple game:
look at the previous tokens, then guess the next token.

At first the model knows nothing.
It sees characters, it makes mistakes, and the loss is high.
After many updates, the model begins to notice patterns:
spaces often follow words, periods often end sentences,
and a question may invite an answer.

This tiny corpus is not enough to make a wise assistant.
It is only a small playground for understanding the machine.
The important lesson is the full loop:
text becomes tokens,
tokens become tensors,
tensors flow through attention,
attention mixes context,
the model predicts logits,
the loss measures surprise,
and gradients adjust the weights.

中文也可以进入这个小语料。
模型不会真正理解世界，
但它会学习字符之间的统计规律。
如果你给它更多干净的数据、更大的模型和更长的训练时间，
它就会逐渐从玩具实验走向更真实的语言模型。

Interview notes:
causal attention prevents future leakage.
embeddings turn token ids into vectors.
the optimizer changes weights using gradients.
validation loss tells us whether the model generalizes.
sampling turns probabilities back into text.
